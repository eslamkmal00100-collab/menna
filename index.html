Cholinesterase AChE & BChE

Enzyme  ·  Synapse  ·  Hydrolysis

A comprehensive clinical study of the biological stop switch governing nerve impulse transmission — its function, tissue origins, diagnostic significance, and laboratory measurement.

Section I

The Function
of This Enzyme

✦

Cholinesterase is a vital enzyme that acts as a "biological stop switch." Its primary function is to catalyze the hydrolysis of the neurotransmitter Acetylcholine (ACh) into Choline and Acetic Acid.

Mechanism: This reaction terminates the transmission of nerve impulses across synapses, preventing continuous muscle contraction or overstimulation of the nervous system.

Hydrolysis Reaction

Acetylcholine (ACh) + H₂O Cholinesterase ⟶ Choline + Acetic Acid

Section II

Tissue Source
Where It Is Synthesized

✦

There are two main types of cholinesterase, each distinguished by their site of synthesis and primary location in the body.

AChE
True Cholinesterase

Primarily found in the Gray matter of the brain, nerve endings, and the membranes of Red Blood Cells (RBCs). It is the principal terminator of cholinergic transmission at the neuromuscular junction.

BChE
Pseudocholinesterase · Butyrylcholinesterase

Synthesized mainly in the Liver and secreted into the Blood Plasma. Also found in the pancreas and heart. Serves as a key biomarker of hepatic synthetic function.

Section III

Diagnostic Significance

✦

Measuring cholinesterase levels is clinically crucial for the following conditions:

☠

Organophosphate Poisoning

Low levels are a definitive diagnostic marker for exposure to pesticides or nerve agents. Inhibition of AChE leads to uncontrolled accumulation of acetylcholine at synapses, producing the characteristic cholinergic toxidrome.

🫀

Liver Function Assessment

Since BChE is produced in the liver, low plasma levels serve as an indicator of chronic liver diseases such as Cirrhosis or Hepatitis, reflecting reduced hepatic synthetic capacity.

⚕

Surgical Safety · Anesthesia

Identifies patients with genetic BChE deficiency who cannot break down muscle relaxants like Succinylcholine, preventing prolonged respiratory paralysis (Apnea) following standard anesthetic doses.

🧠

Monitoring Alzheimer's Disease

Cholinesterase assays are used to follow up on patients receiving cholinesterase inhibitor therapy (Donepezil, Rivastigmine, Galantamine), evaluating pharmacodynamic response and cognitive improvement.

Sections IV & V

Precautions & Sources of Error
Enzyme Assay

✦

To ensure an accurate measurement (Assay), the following must be carefully considered:

🩸

Avoid Hemolysis

If RBCs rupture during sampling, "True Cholinesterase" (AChE) will leak into the plasma, causing a falsely high reading of plasma cholinesterase levels.

🧪

Anticoagulant Choice

Using Fluoride or certain oxalates can inhibit enzyme activity, leading to false low results. Heparin is usually the preferred anticoagulant for cholinesterase assays.

🌡

Sample Stability

Samples must be refrigerated promptly after collection. High temperatures accelerate thermal denaturation, degrading enzyme activity and producing falsely low results.

💊

Patient History

Recent use of oral contraceptives or pregnancy can naturally lower plasma BChE levels slightly. This physiological variation must be accounted for in clinical interpretation.

Section VI

Normal Levels
Reference Ranges

✦

Note: Reference ranges may vary slightly between laboratories.

Plasma · BChE

Pseudocholinesterase

5,300 – 12,900 U/L

Units per Liter — measured in serum or plasma. Reflects hepatic synthetic function and BChE activity.

RBC · AChE

True Cholinesterase

30 – 40 U/g Hb

Units per gram of Hemoglobin — measured in RBC lysate. Used in organophosphate exposure monitoring.

Values significantly below these ranges — particularly in combination with a history of pesticide exposure or relevant clinical symptoms — should prompt immediate further investigation and clinical correlation.
